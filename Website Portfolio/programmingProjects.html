Personal Projects

A few personal projects I have done over the years, outside Astral Dawn Studios

Ludum Dare 44 |
Old Macdonald's Farm

This game was the product of the Ludum Dare 44 GameJam, where 2 friends and I created a game in 72 hours. The game was made around the theme "Life is Currency". This calls, like most GameJams, on your creative ability in design and ingenuity. It was a stressful yet fun experience and this has helped me work under pressure well. I also improved my experience with Tile-Sheets and it is good to get a finished product out there, even if it was a little rushed. This was another example of where learning scope and being realistic is important.

This project was a tower defence game that took a similar format to Plants Vs. Zombies where you would have 5 animals that do different things, as other animals attempted to attack your farm, stealing your livestock, thus draining your currency.

YouTube | Jarkey Bacon |
495,000+ Views | 350+ Subscribers

To be perfectly honest it feels a bit weird talking about my YouTube Channel so publicly. Kids at school got picked on for having a YouTube Channel. While much of it is not a work of art and I don't watch older stuff without cringing, I am not ashamed of my attempts and seeing me grow and progress is incredibly fun. I haven't posted as much since I started University, but I am glad to have this platform to post my thoughts on writing and themes in story telling when I find the time.

I treat it like a photo album of all the fun times I have had playing games with my friends. As for skills learnt, video editing and marketing has been something I have had to learn to get anywhere on YouTube. Also being able to deal with harsh criticism and turn it into something positive is a must to survive on the internet.

EPQ Project | Pincic Pillager

During year 13 of school, I decided to take up a research project that investigates intuitive game design in classic video games such as Super Mario Bros and Mega Man; picking apart the practises of the game developers and looking into opinions of industry experts and game designers of the modern day. From that I created my own game with one level, applying what I learnt into one level, where anyone could sit down and play the game to completion without any verbal instructions. For this project I used Construct 2, and created the game logic, design, art, music and sound FX.

From this project I learnt many things including basic industry practices for planning, how to monitor my own work, and how keep going even when I find it hard. I also learnt how to take feedback and deal with my emotions; not to take criticism too personally and accept the sometimes harsh truth of the situation.
